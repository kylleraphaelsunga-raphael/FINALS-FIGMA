Municipality of Santo Tomas

Where Heritage Meets Industry

HISTORY

Santo Tomas, officially known as the Municipality of Santo Tomas, has a rich history dating back to the late 18th century...

By the early 1800s, Baliwag had become a bustling barrio with a small chapel...

During the late 19th century, Santo Tomas played a role in the Philippine Revolution...

The 20th century brought further development, especially after World War II...

Today, Santo Tomas balances its historical and cultural heritage with modern economic growth...

STORY BEHIND THE NAME

The name "Santo Tomas" comes from Saint Thomas the Apostle, reflecting the town’s deep-rooted Catholic faith...

TRADEMARK OF SANTO TOMAS

Santo Tomas is famous for its casket manufacturing industry, earning the nickname "Casket Capital of Central Luzon"...

INTERESTING FACTS

  • Santo Tomas was originally named "Baliwag" due to the locals’ habit of arriving late for Mass.
  • The town was officially chartered as a municipality on October 12, 1951.
  • The Battle of Santo Tomas in 1899 was a notable event during the Philippine–American War.
  • Santo Tomas is home to over 300 family-owned casket businesses.
  • The town celebrates its patron saint’s feast day on July 3 each year.
