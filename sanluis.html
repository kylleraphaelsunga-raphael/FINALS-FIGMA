Municipality of San Luis

Heart of Central Luzon’s Heritage

HISTORY

San Luis, located in Pampanga, has a rich cultural heritage and abundant natural resources. Established in the early 19th century, it began as a farming and fishing community along the shores of Lawa ng Candaba, which remains vital to the local economy and ecosystem. The town has faced natural calamities but has grown steadily with improved infrastructure connecting it to Central Luzon.

Covering 21.83 square kilometers and comprising 24 barangays, San Luis had a population of approximately 46,800 according to the 2020 census. Its agricultural production includes rice, vegetables, and fishery resources. Local festivals such as the Ati-Atihan Festival and San Luis Fiesta reflect the town’s lively community spirit and deep-rooted traditions.

STORY BEHIND THE NAME

The town was named in honor of San Luis Bertran, a Spanish saint revered during the Spanish colonial period. This reflects the strong religious influence in naming Philippine towns during that era.

LOCAL SIGNIFICANCE

San Luis is known for its productive fishing grounds and fertile agricultural lands, contributing significantly to Pampanga’s economy. The town promotes eco-tourism with attractions such as mangrove forests, wetlands, and local crafts, highlighting its ecological and cultural importance within Central Luzon.

INTERESTING FACTS

  • San Luis is part of the Legazpi Wetlands, important for biodiversity conservation.
  • The town hosts the annual Ati-Atihan Festival with colorful street dances and religious processions.
  • Its coastal location along Lawa ng Candaba influences its fishing-based economy.
  • San Luis participates actively in regional environmental protection programs.
  • The community focuses on sustainable development and cultural preservation.
