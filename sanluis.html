Municipality of San Luis

Heart of Central Luzon’s Heritage

HISTORY

San Luis, located in Pampanga, has a rich cultural heritage and abundant natural resources. Established in the early 19th century, it began as a farming and fishing community along the shores of Lawa ng Candaba, which remains vital to the local economy and ecosystem...

Covering 21.83 square kilometers and comprising 24 barangays, San Luis had a population of approximately 46,800 according to the 2020 census...

STORY BEHIND THE NAME

The town was named in honor of San Luis Bertran, a Spanish saint revered during the Spanish colonial period...

TRADEMARK OF SAN LUIS

San Luis is known for its productive fishing grounds and fertile agricultural lands, contributing significantly to Pampanga’s economy...

INTERESTING FACTS

  • San Luis is part of the Legazpi Wetlands, important for biodiversity conservation.
  • The town hosts the annual Ati-Atihan Festival with colorful street dances and religious processions.
  • Its coastal location along Lawa ng Candaba influences its fishing-based economy.
  • San Luis participates actively in regional environmental protection programs.
  • The community focuses on sustainable development and cultural preservation.
