CITY OF MABALACAT

Gateway to Central Luzon

HISTORY

Mabalacat City was originally a barrio of Bamban, Tarlac before becoming an independent town in 1792. Its earliest settlers were the Aetas, led by their chieftain Caragan, who thrived in the forests where Balacat trees were abundant, inspiring the city’s name. Over time, Kapampangans and migrants from nearby provinces settled in the area, fostering growth and development. By the late 1800s, the town had already established its own church, schools, and barangays, marking its rise as an important Pampanga community. During the American period, a large portion of Mabalacat’s land was turned into Fort Stotsenburg, later known as Clark Air Base, which influenced its economy and culture for decades. The 1991 eruption of Mount Pinatubo prompted the U.S. military’s departure, after which the land was transformed into the Clark Freeport Zone, unlocking new opportunities in trade, tourism, and aviation. On July 21, 2012, Mabalacat was officially converted into a component city of Pampanga through Republic Act No. 10164, making it the province’s third city after Angeles and San Fernando. Today, it thrives as a growing economic hub and is widely recognized as the “Gateway to Central Luzon.”

Mabalacat City has a total land area of about 83.18 square kilometers and is politically subdivided into 27 barangays, including Dau, Mabiga, San Joaquin, Dolores, Sapang Biabas, Camachiles, Tabun, Mamatitang, Poblacion, San Francisco, Atlu Bola, Calumpang, Bical, Bundagul, Dapdap, Lakandula, Marcos Village, Mangalit, San Isidro, San Francisco, and Sapang Balas, among others. According to the 2020 census, the city has a population of more than 293,000 residents, making it one of Pampanga’s most populous areas. It plays a vital role in regional commerce because of its strategic location beside Angeles City and Clark Freeport Zone, and it is directly connected to major road networks like the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX). Educational institutions such as Mabalacat City College and various public and private schools serve its residents, while health facilities and public services continue to expand. The city celebrates local fiestas such as the Fiesta of Our Lady of Grace Parish in Dau and the Feast of San Rafael in Poblacion, showcasing its strong Kapampangan faith and traditions. Mabalacat is also home to key landmarks like the Clark International Airport, portions of the Clark Freeport Zone, and heritage sites such as the San Rafael Archangel Parish Church, all of which highlight its balance of historical significance and modern progress.

STORY BEHIND THE NAME

The name Mabalacat comes from the phrase “Ma-balacat,” which means “full of Balacat trees.” The Balacat tree is strong and resilient, with bark used for medicinal purposes. Because of its abundance in the area, the town took its name from this tree, and it remains a symbol of the city’s identity.

TRADEMARK OF MABALACAT

Mabalacat is best known as the “Gateway to Central Luzon” since it covers most of the Clark Freeport Zone and the Clark International Airport. It is also famous for the Caragan Festival, a colorful celebration every February that honors the Aeta heritage and culture. Another important tradition in Mabalacat is the Pastorella, where Latin hymns are sung during Christmas Masses, preserving a centuries-old practice.

INTERESTING FACTS

  • Mabalacat City is the third city of Pampanga, after Angeles and San Fernando.
  • It is home to the Clark Freeport and Special Economic Zone, a hub for tourism, business, and aviation.
  • The city celebrates the Caragan Festival, named after the Aeta chieftain who first led settlers in the area.
  • Mabalacat is also known for its strong connection to Aeta culture, which is still honored in local traditions and festivals.
  • The city hosts Clark International Airport, making it a major travel gateway in the Philippines.
