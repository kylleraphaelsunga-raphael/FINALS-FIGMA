HISTORY

San Simon, Pampanga, was originally part of Bacolor and officially became an independent municipality in 1771...

In the 20th century, San Simon gradually modernized with the establishment of schools, marketplaces, and infrastructure...

From its beginnings as a riverside settlement to its present role as a thriving municipality, San Simon exemplifies the balance of tradition and progress...

STORY BEHIND THE NAME

The town of San Simon was named in honor of Saint Simon, reflecting the town’s strong Catholic roots...

TRADEMARK OF SAN SIMON

San Simon is known for its agricultural productivity, producing rice, sugarcane, and various vegetables that supply markets in Pampanga and nearby provinces...

INTERESTING FACTS

  • San Simon was originally part of Bacolor before becoming an independent municipality in 1771.
  • The town celebrates its patron saint, Saint Simon, with annual fiestas and religious events.
  • Agriculture remains the backbone of the local economy, with rice and sugarcane as key crops.
  • San Simon actively preserves its heritage through cultural festivals, community programs, and religious traditions.
  • The municipality is located along the Pampanga River, contributing to its fertile lands and rich fishing areas.
